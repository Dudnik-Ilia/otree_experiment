In this experiment, you will provide your estimate for the probability of a specific event occurring. We use the Randomized Scoring Rule mechanism.

What does this mean? Your estimate affects your payout: the more accurate your probability estimate, the better your expected payout.

How it Works

  • You state the probability Y (e.g., 50%) that you believe France will become the European champion.
  • The computer randomly selects a number X between 0 and 100.
  • If Y ≥ X, you get a bonus payout if France actually wins.
  • If Y < X, you win a bonus payout with a probability of X%, regardless of whether France wins.

Why is this Mechanism Used?

This method ensures that being truthful maximizes your chances of receiving better payout.

Example:Suppose you believe France has a 60% chance of winning.

  • You report Y = 60%.
  • The computer randomly picks X = 40 (between 0 and 100).
  • Since 60 ≥ 40, you will get a bonus if France wins.
  • If you had reported an artificially higher probability (e.g., Y = 90%), you would not gain any advantage.

Example: If you understate your probability (e.g., say 30% when you really think it's 60%), you might win less because:

  • If X = 50, but France actually wins, you won't receive the bonus because 30 < 50.
  • However, if you had reported your true belief (60%), you would have won the bonus since 60 ≥ 50 and France won.

This way, the system encourages you to report your real belief rather than guessing.
